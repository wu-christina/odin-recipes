Mapo Tofu (麻婆豆腐) - Taiwanese Style

Credit: It's My Dish

Description

Serves: 4

Mapo Tofu (麻婆豆腐) is a classic Chinese dish from Sichuan Province. The Sichuan Peppercorn and doubanjiang (chili bean sauce) gives it its signature taste. Each region has its own spin on this dish. What makes Taiwanese mapo tofu different is its sweeter and less spicy taste.

This is a dish you can easily add and substitute ingredients to your liking. Traditionally made with ground pork, feel free to use ground chicken, turkey, or beef if that's what you have on hand. Other variations include shitake mushrooms or peas. This recipe is more of a guide. Have fun with it!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground pork
  • 1 box tofu (medium to firm)
  • 1/2 cup doubanjiang (chili bean sauce)
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorn powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 cup broth or water + 1 tbsp of water
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 stalks green onions (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mince garlic and ginger finely
  2. Dice the tofu in medium sized squares
  3. Heat up a medium-sized pan, using medium heat. Add 2 tbsp of oil, stir fry the minced garlic and ginger until aromatic
  4. Add 2 cups of ground pork, cook until ground pork is no longer pink. (Optional: Drain the fat from the pork.)
  5. Add 1/2 cup of the doubanjiag (chili bean sauce) to the pan and mix until it is evenly distributed
  6. Add the Sichuan peppercorn powder
  7. Add the diced tofu and 1 cup of broth or water to the pan
  8. Stir carefully and bring the contents of the pan to a boil
  9. Make a cornstarch slurry by mixing the cornstarch with the 1 tbsp of water
  10. Add the slurry to the pan and mix it in
  11. Remove from heat once the sauce is thickened
  12. Garnish with chopped green onions (optional)
